Minimal residual disease (MRD) is a prognostic indicator

MRD status is a key early warning of clinical relapse{{CitationNumber[1]}}

Relapse may be the result of the continued presence of MRD in patients, despite achieving a hematologic complete remission{{CitationNumber[2]}}

Heterogeneity is a major reason that ALL is difficult to manage{{CitationNumber[3]}},{{CitationNumber[4]}}

● MRD testing can uncover leukemic progenitor cells in patients with a hematologic complete response{{CitationNumber[1]}}
● Evaluation of bone marrow by microscopic morphological testing cannot identify the presence of leukemic cells if there are fewer than 5% (1 in 20) in the total cell population{{CitationNumber[5]}},{{CitationNumber[6]}}
● A substantial proportion of persistent MRD has been reported to consist of slowly dividing progenitor cells that may be resistant to certain therapies{{CitationNumber[4]}},{{CitationNumber[7]}}

MRD testing is routinely performed at specialized treatment centers{{CitationNumber[1]}}
